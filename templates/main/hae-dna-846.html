About the Course

Course Title:

HAE-DNA-846 – Human and Environmental DNA Analysis

Overview:

This advanced-level course is designed to offer a deep dive into the structure, function, and analytical techniques related to human and environmental DNA. Students will explore topics ranging from genetic markers and forensic applications to environmental DNA (eDNA) sampling and bioinformatics.

What You’ll Learn:
  • DNA structure and replication
  • PCR, sequencing, and electrophoresis
  • Forensic DNA analysis
  • Environmental DNA (eDNA) collection and interpretation
  • Bioinformatics tools for DNA data

Curriculum

Module 1
– Introduction to DNA

Module 2
– Laboratory Techniques (PCR, Gel Electrophoresis)

Module 3
– Human Genetics and Forensic Applications

Module 4
– Environmental DNA and Ecological Monitoring

Module 5
– Data Analysis and Bioinformatics

Module 6
– Capstone Project: Real-World DNA Case Study

Instructor

Dr. Elena Moore, PhD

Molecular Biologist and eDNA Specialist with over 15 years of research and teaching experience. Dr. Moore brings both academic and field expertise to guide students through real-world applications of DNA science.

Who Should Enroll

  • Environmental scientists and conservationists
  • Lab technicians and genetic researchers
  • Law enforcement and forensic professionals
  • Curious learners with a science background
